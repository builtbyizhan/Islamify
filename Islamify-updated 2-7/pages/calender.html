Major Islamic Dates

  • Islamic New Year 1 Muharram
    The beginning of the Hijri year, commemorating the migration (Hijrah) of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) from Makkah to Madinah.
    Significance: Marks the start of the Islamic lunar calendar and the Hijrah event.
    Did you know? The Islamic calendar is about 11 days shorter than the Gregorian calendar.
    Learn More
  • Ashura 10 Muharram
    A significant day of fasting and reflection, marking the day Allah saved Moses (Musa) and the Israelites from Pharaoh.
    Significance: Observed by fasting; also commemorates the martyrdom of Husayn ibn Ali in Karbala.
    Did you know? Fasting on Ashura expiates the sins of the previous year.
    Learn More
  • Mawlid an-Nabi 12 Rabi' al-awwal
    The birthday of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), observed in many Muslim communities with prayers and gatherings.
    Significance: Celebrates the birth of the final Prophet of Islam.
    Did you know? Mawlid is celebrated differently across the Muslim world.
    Learn More
  • Start of Ramadan 1 Ramadan
    The beginning of the holy month of fasting, prayer, and reflection for Muslims worldwide.
    Significance: Start of the month of fasting, one of the Five Pillars of Islam.
    Did you know? Ramadan moves through all seasons over a 33-year cycle.
    Learn More
  • Laylat al-Qadr 27 Ramadan
    The Night of Power, believed to be when the Quran was first revealed. Worship on this night is better than a thousand months.
    Significance: The holiest night in Islam, prayers are highly rewarded.
    Did you know? The exact date of Laylat al-Qadr is not known, but it is most commonly observed on the 27th night.
    Learn More
  • Eid al-Fitr 1 Shawwal
    Festival of Breaking the Fast, celebrated at the end of Ramadan with prayers, feasts, and charity.
    Significance: Marks the end of Ramadan and a month of fasting.
    Did you know? Muslims are required to give Zakat al-Fitr before the Eid prayer.
    Learn More
  • Hajj 8-13 Dhu al-Hijjah
    The annual pilgrimage to Makkah, required once in a lifetime for those who are able.
    Significance: One of the Five Pillars of Islam, a spiritual journey of a lifetime.
    Did you know? Over 2 million Muslims perform Hajj every year.
    Learn More
  • Day of Arafah 9 Dhu al-Hijjah
    The most important day of Hajj, a day of fasting and supplication.
    Significance: Standing at Arafah is the essence of Hajj; fasting is highly recommended for non-pilgrims.
    Did you know? The Prophet (PBUH) said, 'Hajj is Arafah.'
    Learn More
  • Eid al-Adha 10 Dhu al-Hijjah
    Festival of Sacrifice, commemorating Prophet Ibrahim's (Abraham) willingness to sacrifice his son for Allah.
    Significance: Celebrated with animal sacrifice, prayers, and charity.
    Did you know? The meat from the sacrifice is distributed among family, friends, and the needy.
    Learn More
