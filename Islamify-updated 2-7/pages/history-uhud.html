Battle of Uhud
Shawwal, 3 AH (625 CE) — Near Mount Uhud, Arabia
“So do not weaken and do not grieve, and you will be superior if you are [true] believers.” - Quran 3:139

Prelude

After the victory at Badr, the Quraysh of Mecca sought revenge. The Muslims of Madinah prepared to defend their city, and the two armies met near Mount Uhud.

“Uhud was a test of resolve and unity for the early Muslims.”

The Armies

The Muslim army numbered about 700, while the Quraysh brought around 3,000 men. The Muslims took up defensive positions at the foot of Mount Uhud.

“Numbers alone do not determine the outcome—faith and discipline matter most.”

The Battle

The Muslims initially had the upper hand, but a breach of orders by some archers allowed the Quraysh to counterattack. The Muslims suffered heavy losses, and the Prophet (PBUH) was injured.

“Obedience and discipline are keys to success.”

Aftermath & Lessons

Despite the setback, the Muslims remained resilient. The lessons learned at Uhud strengthened the community and prepared them for future challenges. The Quran reminded them to remain steadfast and united.

“So do not lose heart and do not grieve, and you will be superior if you are [true] believers.” - Quran 3:139
