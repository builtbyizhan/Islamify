Islamic History Timeline

A journey through the most significant events that shaped the world and the hearts of billions.

“Indeed, this is a reminder, so whoever wills may take to his Lord a way.” - Quran 73:19

Life of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH)

The Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) was born in Mecca in 570 CE, marking the beginning of a new era in human history.

First Revelation

In 610 CE, Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) received the first revelation from Allah through Angel Jibreel in the Cave of Hira.

Hijrah (Migration to Madinah)

In 622 CE, the Prophet and his followers migrated from Mecca to Madinah, marking the start of the Islamic calendar (Hijri).

Battle of Badr

The first major battle in Islam, fought in 624 CE, where Muslims achieved a decisive victory against the Quraysh of Mecca.

Battle of Uhud

Fought in 625 CE near Mount Uhud, this battle was a test of faith and unity for the early Muslim community.

Treaty of Hudaybiyyah

In 628 CE, a peace treaty was signed between the Muslims and Quraysh, paving the way for the spread of Islam.

Conquest of Makkah

In 630 CE, Muslims peacefully conquered Makkah, leading to the widespread acceptance of Islam in Arabia.

Farewell Sermon

During his final pilgrimage in 632 CE, the Prophet delivered his Farewell Sermon, emphasizing equality, justice, and brotherhood.

Compilation of the Quran

After the Prophet's passing, the Quran was compiled into a single book during the caliphate of Abu Bakr and Uthman (RA).

Spread of Islam

Islam rapidly spread beyond Arabia, reaching Africa, Asia, and Europe, shaping civilizations and cultures worldwide.
