Five Pillars of Islam

The foundation of Muslim life and faith

1. Shahada (Declaration of Faith)

The Shahada is the first and most important pillar of Islam. It is the declaration of faith that every Muslim must make to enter the fold of Islam.

أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَأَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ

Translation: "I bear witness that there is no god but Allah, and I bear witness that Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah."

Importance

  • It is the foundation of Islamic belief
  • It distinguishes a Muslim from a non-Muslim
  • It must be said with sincerity and understanding
  • It should be recited in Arabic

Conditions of Shahada

  • Knowledge (Ilm): Understanding its meaning
  • Certainty (Yaqeen): Being certain of its truth
  • Sincerity (Ikhlas): Saying it purely for Allah
  • Truthfulness (Sidq): Being truthful in saying it
  • Love (Mahabbah): Loving Allah and His Messenger
  • Acceptance (Qubool): Accepting its requirements
  • Submission (Inqiyad): Submitting to its commands
Reference: Sahih al-Bukhari 8, Sahih Muslim 19

2. Salah (Prayer)

Salah is the second pillar of Islam and is the daily prayer that Muslims perform five times a day.

Timing of Prayers

  • Fajr: Before sunrise
  • Dhuhr: After midday
  • Asr: Afternoon
  • Maghrib: After sunset
  • Isha: Night

Importance

  • Direct connection with Allah
  • Purification of the soul
  • Discipline and time management
  • Unity among Muslims

Requirements for Prayer

  • Cleanliness (Wudu)
  • Clean clothes and place
  • Facing the Qibla
  • Proper intention
  • Covering the awrah

Steps of Prayer

  1. Takbir (Opening)
  2. Qiyam (Standing)
  3. Ruku (Bowing)
  4. Sujood (Prostration)
  5. Tashahhud (Sitting)
  6. Tasleem (Ending)
Reference: Quran 2:238, Sahih al-Bukhari 8

3. Zakat (Charity)

Zakat is the third pillar of Islam and is the obligatory charity that Muslims must give to those in need.

Conditions for Zakat

  • Being Muslim
  • Being free
  • Owning the Nisab (minimum amount)
  • Complete ownership
  • Passing of one lunar year

Nisab (Minimum Amount)

  • Gold: 85 grams
  • Silver: 595 grams
  • Cash: Equivalent to above
  • Rate: 2.5% of total wealth

Recipients of Zakat

  • The poor
  • The needy
  • Those employed to collect Zakat
  • Those whose hearts are to be reconciled
  • To free slaves
  • Those in debt
  • In the cause of Allah
  • Wayfarers
Reference: Quran 9:60, Sahih al-Bukhari 1395

4. Sawm (Fasting)

Sawm is the fourth pillar of Islam and refers to fasting during the month of Ramadan.

Types of Fasting

  • Obligatory (Ramadan)
  • Recommended (Mondays and Thursdays)
  • Prohibited (Eid days)

Requirements for Fasting

  • Intention
  • Abstaining from food and drink
  • Abstaining from marital relations
  • From dawn to sunset

Who is Exempt?

  • Children (before puberty)
  • The elderly
  • The sick
  • Travelers
  • Pregnant women
  • Nursing mothers

Benefits of Fasting

  • Spiritual purification
  • Self-discipline
  • Empathy for the poor
  • Health benefits
  • Increased taqwa (God-consciousness)
Reference: Quran 2:183-185, Sahih al-Bukhari 1900

5. Hajj (Pilgrimage)

Hajj is the fifth pillar of Islam and is the pilgrimage to Makkah that every able Muslim must perform at least once in their lifetime.

Conditions for Hajj

  • Being Muslim
  • Being of sound mind
  • Being an adult
  • Being financially able
  • Being physically able
  • Having a mahram (for women)

Main Rituals

  1. Ihram (Entering the state of pilgrimage)
  2. Tawaf (Circling the Kaaba)
  3. Sa'i (Walking between Safa and Marwa)
  4. Standing at Arafat
  5. Stoning the Jamarat
  6. Sacrifice
  7. Shaving/Cutting hair

Benefits of Hajj

  • Complete forgiveness of sins
  • Spiritual renewal
  • Unity of Muslims
  • Following the footsteps of Prophet Ibrahim
  • Increased taqwa
Reference: Quran 3:97, Sahih al-Bukhari 1521
