Conquest of Makkah
630 CE — Makkah, Arabia
“When the victory of Allah has come and the conquest, and you see the people entering into the religion of Allah in multitudes...” - Quran 110:1-2

Prelude

After the Quraysh broke the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah, the Prophet (PBUH) led a large Muslim army to Makkah. The city surrendered peacefully, and the Prophet (PBUH) forgave his former enemies.

“Mercy and forgiveness triumphed over vengeance.”

The Event

The Kaaba was cleansed of idols and rededicated to Allah. The people of Makkah embraced Islam in large numbers, marking a new era for the Arabian Peninsula.

“The conquest was peaceful, setting a precedent for compassion in victory.”

Significance

The conquest marked the triumph of Islam in Arabia. The Prophet's (PBUH) mercy and leadership inspired many to accept Islam.

“True strength is shown through mercy and humility.”

Legacy

The event set a precedent for mercy and forgiveness in victory. It paved the way for the rapid spread of Islam across the Arabian Peninsula.

“The legacy of Makkah is one of peace, unity, and faith.”

Sources

  • Quran, Surah Al-Nasr (110:1-3)
  • Sahih Bukhari, Book 59, Hadith 582
  • Martin Lings, "Muhammad: His Life Based on the Earliest Sources"
